Wine & Châteaux

Vineyard visits, tastings, and medieval castles

Lasclottes sits at a crossroads of some of France's most celebrated wine regions. From the nearby vineyards of AOC Buzet to the famous Malbec of Cahors and the diverse wines of Bergerac, there are world-class tastings within easy reach. Combine them with visits to spectacular medieval châteaux for a perfect day out.

Wine Regions Near Lasclottes

AOC Buzet

35–40 minutes from Lasclottes

The nearest wine appellation, producing excellent reds, rosés, and whites from Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Cabernet Franc grapes. The cooperative Les Vignerons de Buzet offers tastings and tours of their cellars, with wines available to buy direct at very reasonable prices. A great introduction to the wines of southwest France.

Cahors

1 hour 10 minutes from Lasclottes

Famous worldwide for its deep, rich Malbec wines — the "black wine of Cahors" has been produced here since Roman times. The vineyards line the Lot Valley upstream, and many domaines welcome visitors for tastings. The town of Cahors itself is beautiful, with a medieval bridge and excellent restaurants.

Bergerac & Dordogne

1 hour from Lasclottes

The Bergerac appellation produces a wide range of wines from robust reds to sweet Monbazillac dessert wines. Many châteaux offer tastings in beautiful settings. Combine a wine tour with a visit to the charming old town of Bergerac on the Dordogne river.

Côtes de Duras

45 minutes from Lasclottes

A smaller, less-known appellation producing excellent value wines. The vineyards sit on rolling hills between the Lot-et-Garonne and the Dordogne, and the small-scale producers are particularly welcoming to visitors. Great for discovering hidden gems.

Châteaux to Visit

The region is rich in medieval architecture. These châteaux make excellent day trips, and combine perfectly with a vineyard visit on the same outing.

Château de Bonaguil

25 km from Lasclottes • 30 minutes

One of the last great medieval castles built in France, completed in the late 15th century. Dramatically perched on a rocky spur, it was designed to withstand artillery — yet never actually attacked. Guided tours and summer events make this a must-visit. An astonishing sight rising above the forest.

Château de Biron

40 km from Lasclottes • 45 minutes

A magnificent castle that has evolved over 800 years, combining Romanesque, Gothic, and Renaissance architecture. Set in the Dordogne countryside near the beautiful village of Biron, it offers panoramic views and fascinating tours through centuries of history.

Château de Gavaudun

19 km from Lasclottes • 25 minutes

A dramatic ruined castle perched on a cliff overlooking the Lède Valley. The 12th-century keep still stands tall, offering views from the top. The village below has a small museum and café. A quick but impressive visit easily combined with other outings.

Practical Information

Tastings

Most vineyards offer free tastings (dégustation gratuite). It's polite to buy a bottle or two if you enjoy the wines. Larger cooperatives have set opening hours; smaller domaines may need a call ahead.

Designated Driver

Wine tasting and driving don't mix. Consider splitting driving duties within your group, or use the spittoons provided at tastings. French drink-driving limits are strict (0.5g/l).

Best Time

Vineyards are open year-round but are at their most atmospheric during harvest (September–October). Summer has the longest opening hours. Châteaux may have reduced hours outside July–August.

Buying Wine

Buying direct from the producer is excellent value. Many estates offer cases at discounted prices. Stock up the holiday house — you'll pay a fraction of UK wine-shop prices.
